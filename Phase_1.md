# Trading Bot Implementation

This project applies nature-inspired optimisation techniques to algorithmic trading. A parameterised strategy using technical indicators (e.g., moving averages) is evaluated on historical Bitcoin OHLCV data, with population-based methods used to optimise parameters through backtesting and comparative analysis.

---

# Phase 1: Nature-Inspired Optimisation for Trading Bots

## Overview
This phase focuses on exploring nature-inspired optimisation algorithms and evaluating their suitability for algorithmic trading. The goal is to understand how these algorithms operate, their strengths and limitations, and how they can be applied to optimise trading strategies.

---

## Objectives
- Investigate population-based optimisation algorithms  
- Understand their design principles and behaviour  
- Analyse their strengths, weaknesses, and novelty  
- Compare multiple algorithms to determine their suitability for:
  - Parameter optimisation  
  - Trading strategy tuning  

---

## Dataset
We utilise the **Nature-Inspired Algorithms dataset (Tzanetos, 2020)**, which contains:

- ~300 optimisation algorithms  
- Categorised by:
  - Swarm Intelligence  
  - Organism-based methods  
  - Physical phenomena  
  - Other inspirations  
- Metadata including:
  - Year of publication  
  - Application domains  
  - Research impact
**Look through Algorithms Folder** for more details of about these algorithmns

### Purpose of Dataset
- Explore algorithm categories  
- Identify well-established methods  
- Support justification of selected algorithms  

---

## Methodology

Each member:

1. Selects one optimisation algorithm  
2. Conducts a literature review  
3. Writes a **1–1.5 page synopsis** addressing:
   - Problem addressed by the algorithm  
   - Limitations of previous approaches  
   - Key idea / novelty  
   - Methodology and implementation  
   - Results and validation  
   - Critical evaluation
   - 
**Look through Project description** for more details about Deliverables  

---

## Algorithm Comparison

After individual analyses, a joint comparison is conducted:

- Identify similarities and differences  
- Compare:
  - Exploration vs exploitation  
  - Convergence behaviour  
  - Suitability for optimisation tasks  
- Evaluate applicability to trading strategy optimisation  

---

## Deliverables
- Individual synopses (per team member)  
- Comparative analysis (1–2 pages)  

**Total report length:** ~5–8 pages  

---

## Connection to Phase 2

The insights from this phase will guide:

- Selection of algorithms for implementation  
- Design of optimisation strategies  
- Development of an AI trading bot  

---

## Key Outcome

By the end of Phase 1, the team will have:

- A strong understanding of nature-inspired optimisation  
- A justified selection of algorithms  
- A foundation for building and optimising a trading bot in Phase 2
